    Worksheet Math 121 Week 5 Activity
    

    Objectives

    • Find values of the sine and cosine functions.
      🔗
      🔗
    • Translate between radians and degrees
      🔗
      🔗
    • Apply transformations to change the amplitude and midline of sinusoidal functions.
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗
    Please feel free to work together. If you get stuck, just ask and we’ll get unstuck.
    🔗
    Please note, some of these questions have answers that will require you to write complete sentences in order to help you make better sense of the math you’re learning. This may seem difficult at first.
    🔗

    1.

    Recall the following relationship between radians and degrees:
    \begin{equation*} 1 \text{radian} = \frac{\pi}{180} degrees. \end{equation*}
    🔗

    (a)

    🔗
    Draw the points on the unit circle at \(\theta = \pi/6, \pi/3, \pi/4, \) and any other points whose reference angles equal \(\pi/3, \pi/4, \) or \(\pi/6. \)
    🔗
    Note: There should be 12 total points.
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    (b)

    Now, for each of these labeled points, write a corresponding angle measured counter-clockwise from the positive \(x\)-axis and the sine and cosine of this angle. (Making a table might be a good idea.)
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    2.

    Suppose a mill is powered by a waterwheel on a river with a horizontal axle. The wheel has radius \(10 \) feet and the water rises \(5 \) feet above the bottom the wheel.
    🔗

    (a)

    Write an equation for the function \(H\) which gives the height on the edge of the wheel as a function of the angle \(\theta \) measured counterclockwise from 3 o’clock on the wheel. Sketch a graph of this function.
    🔗
    🔗

    (b)

    Now suppose the wheel takes \(30\) seconds to perform one full rotation. If you hang onto the edge of the wheel and ride one full rotation, how much time would you spend at least \(5+10\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \) feet above the water?
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    3.

    In this problem, we have the same waterwheel as in the last problem. Now we’ll think about arc length and angles.
    🔗

    (a)

    What is the arc length (measured on the edge of the wheel) that is underwater?
    🔗
    🔗

    (b)

    If the water is flowing at the same speed as the wheel (i.e. the water moves 1 foot in the same time it takes for the outside of the wheel to rotate \(1 \) foot), how fast is the river moving (in feet per second).
    🔗
