    Worksheet Math 121 Week 7 Activity
    

    Objectives

    • Use trig and inverse trig functions to find sides and angles on right triangles
      🔗
      🔗
    • Apply right triangle trigonometry to practical applications
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗
    Please feel free to work together. If you get stuck, just ask and we’ll get unstuck.
    🔗
    Please note, some of these questions have answers that will require you to write complete sentences in order to help you make better sense of the math you’re learning. This may seem difficult at first.
    🔗

    1.

    Suppose car A is 30 miles due North of an intersection traveling towards it and car B is 50 miles due East of the intersection traveling towards it.
    🔗

    (a)

    What angle would car A have to turn (in degrees) so that it is pointed directly at car B?
    🔗
    🔗

    (b)

    If car A stays put, how far would car B have to travel for this angle to become 20 degrees?
    🔗
    🔗

    (c)

    If car B stays put, how far would car A have to travel for this angle to become 70 degrees?
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    2.

    Suppose you ride an escalator that is moving at 5 feet per second. The escalator is 50 feet tall and takes 20 seconds to ride from bottom to top.
    🔗

    (a)

    How long is the escalator? (this should be the hypotenuse of a triangle)
    🔗
    🔗

    (b)

    What is the angle of ascent of the escalator?
    🔗
    🔗

    (c)

    How many vertical feet does the escalator gain per second?
    🔗
    🔗

    (d)

    How many horizontal feet does the escalator traverse per second?
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    3.

    Suppose you ski down a slope at a fixed angle that drops 1000 feet over a horizontal displacement of 3500 feet.
    🔗

    (a)

    What is the angle of the slope from horizontal?
    🔗
    🔗

    (b)

    If satellite data measures your horizontal speed to be \(25 \) feet per second, how quickly are you moving across the snow?
    🔗
    🔗

    (c)

    At the speed from part b, how quickly are you losing elevation?
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗
